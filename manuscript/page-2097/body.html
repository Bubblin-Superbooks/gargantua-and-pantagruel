And my sweet wife shall hold the combat Long as my baws can on her bum beat. O what a battle of a— fighting Will there be, which I much delight in! What pleasing pains then shall I take To keep myself and spouse awake! All heart and juice, I’ll up and ride, And make a duchess of my bride. Sing Io paean! loudly sing To Hymen, who all joys will bring. Well, Friar John, I’ll take my oath, This oracle is full of troth; Intelligible truth it bears, More certain than the sieve and shears.
